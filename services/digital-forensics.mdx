## Evidence preservation standards

All evidence acquisition and handling follows established forensic standards:

- Bit-for-bit imaging of storage media using write-blocking hardware
- Hash verification (MD5/SHA-256) at acquisition and all subsequent steps to demonstrate integrity
- Chain of custody documentation maintained from first contact through report delivery
- Analysis conducted on forensic copies only — original evidence is never altered

## When to engage digital forensics

Digital forensics services are typically appropriate when:

- A security incident has occurred and you need to understand what happened, how, and what was accessed or exfiltrated
- An employee termination or internal investigation involves alleged misuse of company systems or data
- You are preparing for or responding to legal proceedings that involve digital evidence
- A regulatory body or legal process requires documented evidence from your systems
