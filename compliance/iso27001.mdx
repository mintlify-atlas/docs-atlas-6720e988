ISO/IEC 27001:2022 is the international standard that defines how organizations should build, operate, and continually improve an Information Security Management System (ISMS). For organizations operating in Mexico — whether serving government clients, handling sensitive financial data, or competing for enterprise contracts — ISO 27001 certification signals that information security is governed systematically rather than reactively. ByteWise holds the certifications and practical experience to guide organizations through every phase of the journey.

## What is ISO/IEC 27001:2022

ISO/IEC 27001 is published by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC). It specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an ISMS within the context of an organization's risk environment.

Certification is granted by accredited third-party audit bodies after a two-stage audit process — a documentation review followed by an on-site assessment of controls in practice. Certified organizations undergo annual surveillance audits and a full recertification audit every three years.

The 2022 revision restructured the Annex A controls from 114 to 93 controls organized into four themes: organizational, people, physical, and technological. Organizations certified under the 2013 edition had until October 2025 to transition.

<Note>
  ISO 27001 certification applies to a defined scope — not necessarily the entire organization. ByteWise helps you define a scope that is meaningful to clients and auditors while remaining achievable within your timeline.
</Note>

## Who needs ISO 27001 certification

ISO 27001 certification is required or strongly expected in several contexts:

- **Government and public-sector contracts**: Many federal and state procurement processes in Mexico require suppliers to demonstrate documented information security controls.
- **Financial services and fintech**: Regulated entities and their technology providers face increasing scrutiny from the CNBV and Banco de México regarding vendor security practices.
- **Healthcare and insurance**: Organizations handling clinical or actuarial data need to demonstrate that confidentiality and integrity controls are formally managed.
- **Enterprise supply chains**: Large corporations increasingly require ISO 27001 certification as a condition for onboarding technology and data-processing vendors.
- **Organizations seeking competitive differentiation**: Certification demonstrates security maturity to prospects who evaluate risk as part of their vendor selection process.

Even where certification is not contractually required, the discipline of implementing an ISMS — risk assessments, asset classification, documented procedures, and management review — produces measurable improvements in security posture.

## ByteWise's approach

ByteWise follows a structured engagement model aligned to the ISO 27001 implementation lifecycle. Each phase has defined deliverables so you know exactly where you are and what comes next.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Maturity assessment">
    ByteWise conducts an initial evaluation of your current security practices against the ISO 27001 requirements. This produces a baseline maturity score and highlights the areas that require the most attention before controls implementation begins.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Gap analysis and risk treatment plan">
    A formal gap analysis maps your current state against all Annex A controls and the standard's clauses. ByteWise then works with your team to build a risk register, assess likelihood and impact for identified threats, and select appropriate controls for risk treatment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Controls implementation">
    ByteWise supports drafting and implementing the policies, procedures, and technical controls required by your chosen scope. This includes access control policies, asset inventory processes, supplier agreements, incident management procedures, and business continuity plans.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Staff training and awareness">
    People are the most common source of security incidents. ByteWise delivers targeted training sessions so that employees at all levels understand their responsibilities under the ISMS and can recognize phishing, social engineering, and improper data handling.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Internal audit">
    Before the certification audit, ByteWise performs a full internal audit of your ISMS — identifying nonconformities, verifying that controls are operating as intended, and preparing corrective action records. This rehearsal significantly reduces the risk of surprises during the external audit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Certification preparation">
    ByteWise accompanies your management team through the Stage 1 documentation review and Stage 2 on-site audit conducted by the accredited certification body. This includes preparing your management for interviews, organizing evidence packages, and responding to auditor observations.
  </Step>
</Steps>
