ByteWise does not apply generic templates or deliver isolated reports. Every engagement follows a structured four-phase methodology designed to produce measurable, lasting results — calibrated to your organization's actual security maturity, operational context, and goals. The process begins with deep diagnostic work and ends with ongoing partnership, not a handoff.

## The four phases

<Steps>
  <Step title="Deep diagnostic">
    ByteWise conducts a thorough assessment of your organization's current security posture. This includes a maturity evaluation, gap analysis against ISO/IEC 27001:2022 controls, identification and classification of critical assets, and a clear picture of active risks and compliance shortfalls. The output is an honest, prioritized baseline — not a sales document.

    <Info>
      The initial diagnostic is offered at no cost. You receive a written analysis of gaps, risks, and priorities before any further commitment is required.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Strategic plan">
    Based on the diagnostic findings, ByteWise develops a tailored action plan. This plan defines the controls to implement, the policies and procedures to establish, and the sequence of work — ordered by risk priority and organizational feasibility. The plan aligns with your chosen service tier and internal capacity.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phased implementation">
    Controls, policies, processes, and technical measures are implemented in structured phases rather than all at once. This approach reduces disruption to operations, allows your team to absorb changes progressively, and enables course corrections along the way. Implementation may include technical hardening, documentation, personnel training, and awareness programs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Continuous accompaniment">
    ByteWise remains engaged after initial implementation. This means ongoing support for monitoring, incident response readiness, policy updates as your environment evolves, and preparation for internal or external audits. The relationship is designed to grow with your organization — not to conclude with a final report.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Service tiers

ByteWise structures engagements across three tiers, allowing organizations to enter the process at the level appropriate for their current maturity and resource availability.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Basic" icon="circle-1">
    **Diagnostic and action plan**

    Ideal for organizations starting from zero or looking to formalize their security program. Includes a full maturity assessment, gap analysis, asset inventory, and a prioritized remediation roadmap.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Intermediate" icon="circle-2">
    **Controls implementation and risk management**

    For organizations ready to act on their diagnostic findings. Includes implementation of technical and administrative controls, policy development, risk treatment, and staff awareness training.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Advanced" icon="circle-3">
    **Internal audit and certification preparation**

    For organizations targeting ISO/IEC 27001 certification. Includes internal ISMS audit, evidence review, pre-certification gap closure, and direct preparation for the external certification audit.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  Organizations do not need to know which tier is right for them before reaching out. The free diagnostic phase will make the appropriate starting point clear based on your actual situation.
</Tip>

## Why this approach works

Most cybersecurity engagements fail not because of technical deficiency but because solutions are not matched to organizational reality. ByteWise's phased methodology addresses this directly:

- The diagnostic phase ensures recommendations are based on evidence, not assumptions
- The strategic plan translates findings into actionable steps your team can execute
- Phased implementation prevents change fatigue and preserves operational continuity
- Continuous accompaniment means your security posture keeps pace with new threats and business changes
